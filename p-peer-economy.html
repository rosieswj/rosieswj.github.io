UX Design: Responsible Web for Peer Economy

View Process book

Role - UX Design, Prototyping using InVision

Peer economy grows rapidly with the fast development of technology. Some of the most well-known examples today include Uber (car rides), Airbnb (hotels), and Etsy (craft goods), which are much faster and cheaper compare to traditional service providers. The key to such services is a across-device platform for peer interactions.

In this situation, we were given the problem of inefficient shift-swapping system for workers and managers at restaurants. We aimed to solve issues such as delayed communication, unfilled time slots, lack of incentives, etc.

We started our design process by writing scenarios which illustrate a co-production of values for different user groups. We then proceeded to making key interaction scenarios using storyboards. Through several rounds of refining wireframes, we developed a prototype for both mobile and laptop of the website using InVision.

In this project, I was involved in writing scenario writing and making storyboards. I was responsible for the style guide for our interface, and I worked with 2 of my teammates primarily on the desktop design of the responsive web.
