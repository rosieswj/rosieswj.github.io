My name is Rosie and I'm a senior studying Computer Science and Human-Computer Interaction @ Carnegie Mellon.

I’m always passionate to use technology to design and develop products and services that enhance people’s real-world experiences. Today’s technology is powerful for its fast-growing intelligence and the ability to carry out tasks at unprecedented efficiency. But to me, the real power of technology is in how it is redefining the way we as humans think, behave and interact with each other. In some cases such influence is not necessarily positive. Technology may pull us away from our real feelings or isolate individuals. It's my goal to make new technology accessible and friendly and to ultimately deliver a good, authentic experience to people. [Thanks for coming to my Ted talk :)]

My current interests are software development, object-oriented programming and user experience design for digital services. My research @ Human-Computer Interaction Institute focuses on user privacy and security.

In my free time I enjoy watching social and cultural documentaries and reading. I also spend too much time on Spotify:-D
