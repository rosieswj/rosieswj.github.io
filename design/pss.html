Sephora Product-Service System

Skills: Visual Design, Service Deconstruction, Systems Thinking
Time Span: September - October 2018

Overview

In this case study, I explored how a specific combination of products and services interact to generate value for the relevant stakeholders. In particular, I studied how Sephora as a beauty retail giant uses innovative servization to seek new opportunities in a traditionally product-dominated company. My research process includes:
1. Deconstruct a product-service system to identify key components and stakeholders, and the relationships between them.
2. Scope down the service by focusing on a particular innovation, and how it interacts with other components in the system through unbundling, rebundling, dematerialization, etc.
3. Idenitify how value is created through the innovation, and create a poster documenting my finding.

Phase 1: Brainstorming

I first selected three product-service systems that are either a service provider or a product-dominated company:

  • Sephora and its transition from a retail store to a service provider
  • Lululemon and its focus on building strong brand loyality and ties to customers
  • Spotify and how it revolutionizes the music streaming service
